    Open source solar PV inverter design

    Photovoltaic (PV) energy is emerging as one of the mainstream power sources. In Sri Lanka, imported inverters are used for the grid connection of PV plants. The proprietary inverters do not allow accessing control functions implemented already. As a result, the utility does not have the flexibility to introduce even the essential grid supporting functions required on these inverters. In this project, a single-phase open-source solar PV inverter is developed providing the flexibility to access and edit grid supporting functions required to integrate the PV system to distribution network in the grid. The inverter and boost converter is designed, hardware-implemented, and tested. Since this is a smart device, various MPPT algorithms, active power control functions, reactive power control functions can be introduced in the boost converter and in the inverter, respectively.

    Change over arrangement for grid connected inverter to off-grid of solar PV system

    Hardware setup of the Proposed design

    Introduction

    • Basically, the grid-tied inverters are designed to power off when there is a utility power outage.
    • At daytime, these types of inverters create a major drawback when solar generation is maximum.
    • To overcome this drawback, this research study designed a changeover arrangement for grid-connected inverters to operate in off-grid mode in domestic applications.
    • The designed system is capable of utilizing solar energy effectively and thereby providing cost-effective solutions to power-related issues.

    Objectives

    • A changeover arrangement is provided to operate home appliances in a solar PV plant during a grid interruption.
    • The system mainly focuses on operating essential loads like computers, WiFi routers, and televisions without interruption when there is a power outage.
    • A change-over arrangement comprising of:
      • A number of solid-state relays, a microcontroller board, a dump load, and a remote-controlled mains socket.
      • The relays are connected to the grid, the PV inverter, a UPS, and a dump load.
